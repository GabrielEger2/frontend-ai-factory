# Team Component Ideas

Current team category has 3 components, all solving the same problem (show a few faces + bios):
- TeamFounderSplit — founder photo + story, 50/50 split
- TeamLeadershipGrid — uniform grid of leadership headshots
- TeamMemberSpotlight — single featured member with detail card

The gap: nothing for culture, hiring, or journey/storytelling. Three proposals to widen the category.

---

## 1. TeamCultureMosaic

**Purpose:** culture/values showcase — less corporate, more human. For brands selling lifestyle, agencies, restaurants, creative studios, places where "who we are" matters more than "who runs us."

**Layout:** Asymmetric photo mosaic (mix of portrait + landscape + square crops, irregular grid à la Pinterest / editorial mood-board) interleaved with 2-3 short value cards. Photos are lifestyle/candid (people working, laughing, eating together) — NOT polished headshots.

**Slots:**
- `eyebrow` (text, ≤30) — e.g. "How we work"
- `headline` (text, ≤80)
- `intro` (text, ≤200)
- `images[]` (image, 6-9 entries, mixed aspect ratios)
- `values[]` (list of {title ≤40, description ≤140}, 2-4 entries) — interleaved into the mosaic
- `cta_text` (text, ≤30, optional) — "See open roles" / "Meet the team"
- `cta_url` (url, optional)

**Style/mood:** editorial, playful, modern / friendly, fun, energetic
**Pairs well with:** hero-bakery-editorial, hero-polaroid-collage, content galleries
**Pairs poorly with:** corporate hero variants, sticky cta banners

---

## 2. TeamJourneyTimeline

**Purpose:** story-driven team component — founding story + milestones + the people behind each chapter. For founder-led brands, agencies with a strong narrative, family businesses, anything where the story IS the trust signal.

**Layout:** Vertical timeline (alternating left/right or single rail with year markers) where each milestone has: year, headline, short paragraph, and optionally a portrait of the person who joined/led that chapter. Last entry is "Today" with a small group photo or current team grid.

**Slots:**
- `eyebrow` (text, ≤30) — "Our story" / "Since 2014"
- `headline` (text, ≤80)
- `milestones[]` (list, 4-7 entries) of:
  - `year` (text, ≤10)
  - `title` (text, ≤60)
  - `description` (text, ≤220)
  - `image` (image, optional, portrait 4:5)
  - `person_name` (text, ≤40, optional)
  - `person_role` (text, ≤40, optional)
- `today_image` (image, optional) — current team photo for final node

**Style/mood:** editorial, classic, modern / trustworthy, professional, calm
**Pairs well with:** hero variants with brand-statement purpose, testimonial-spotlight-quote, stats-count-up
**Pairs poorly with:** other long-narrative content blocks (avoid stacking two timelines)

---

## 3. TeamHiringCallout

**Purpose:** team page that doubles as a recruiting funnel. For growing startups, agencies, anywhere "we're hiring" is a primary CTA. Shows a few key team members but the focus is the open-roles list and "why work here" pitch.

**Layout:** Split — left side is a compact 2x2 or 3x2 grid of team headshots (smaller than TeamLeadershipGrid, no individual bios — just name + role on hover/tap), right side is a stacked list of open roles with location/department tags + apply CTA per role. Below the split, a single "perks" strip (3-5 icon + label items: remote, equity, health, etc.).

**Slots:**
- `headline` (text, ≤80) — "Build with us" / "We're hiring"
- `subheadline` (text, ≤180)
- `team_members[]` (list, 4-6 entries) of:
  - `name` (text, ≤40)
  - `role` (text, ≤40)
  - `image` (image, square)
- `roles[]` (list, 3-8 entries) of:
  - `title` (text, ≤60)
  - `department` (text, ≤30)
  - `location` (text, ≤30) — "Remote" / "São Paulo" / "Hybrid"
  - `apply_url` (url)
- `perks[]` (list of {icon ≤20, label ≤40}, 3-5 entries)
- `cta_text` (text, ≤30) — "See all roles"
- `cta_url` (url)

**Style/mood:** modern, bold, corporate / energetic, friendly, professional
**Pairs well with:** stats-kpi-grid (company-at-a-glance numbers above), testimonial-video-card (employee testimonials), footer-mega
**Pairs poorly with:** other CTA-heavy blocks immediately adjacent (compete for the apply click)

---

## Recommendation

Build **TeamCultureMosaic** first — it's the highest leverage. The current three team components all assume a polished B2B SaaS context, and the mosaic unlocks the entire creative/lifestyle/hospitality client base that currently has nothing usable here. Journey + Hiring are valuable but more situational.
